Mission Impossible: Less than 5 weeks to prepare, launch and succeed a $120k crowdfunding campaign

Are you a Montreal crowdfund fan? Join us at Crowdfund Night Nov 26th

THE BACK-STORY

Back in late September, we received an invitation from Notman House to film a video.

(N.B: “we” in the whole article refers to Seeding Factory team + VC-TV members + some other Notman community members)

The situation was dire at Montreal’s home of the web: $100k was needed to bridge financing. They have already done a lot of work for the past year getting government grants and also securing contributions from successful entrepreneurs and friends in Montreal. However, there was still $100,000 missing to get a loan from the bank, buy the house and the hospital, and begin renovations.

Gabriel Sundaram, one of the Notman volunteers, thought having an Indiegogo campaign would get those missing funds, a brightÂ entrepreneurialÂ idea. Usually, we need at least 4 weeks to prepare a campaign, from estimating if the project is solid, if there’s any traction and any community to back the project, write a script, shoot a video, and do the pre-marketing campaign. Those are essential steps in making a successful crowdfunding campaign.

QUICK PREPARATIONS

What made us accept the collaboration was the fact that there was a vibrant community we could reach out easily, there were different media (Facebook, twitter, blogs, press) that we were already in touch with. $100,000 seemed a lot of money, however there was real urgency that could be communicated, with a project with tangible perks, and we concluded it was possible after all.

We focused from the beginning on the video. Instead of having a conceptual video with an artistic or fun angle, we chose instead to get as many community leaders as possible and hear how they love Notman House. Everyone came in, happy that we reached out to them. Of course, we got the founders of the project (Alan MacIntosh and John Stokes) to tell in their own words what the Notman House project was all about. We also had a tour of the project in the second part of the video. We also had the excellent idea of having someone from Heritage Montreal telling the story of the house, and the legacy.

The video was produced by a team from HeriPhotography and StudioDuPlateau

We also made sure we had the right keywords in the title. “HELP BUILD NOTMAN” was displayed everywhere in the video. It’s a great tagline and vision that everyone wants to be part of.

A video such as above requires 5 people in the production team (photography, audio, lights, producer, project management). We had also 2 interns working on files and various tasks, as well as the Notman team who were facilitation the work and preparing the launch.

A GREAT START

For the launch, we had the page up and immediately we:
– announced the campaign on 2 facebook groups
– asked everyone involved in the campaign to share on their personal facebook/twitter
– had 2 blog posts, one on MontrealTechWatch and one on Techvibes
– and announced the campaign on 3 different mailing-lists.

The first day of the campaign was thrilling. In 24 hours, we had $25k, with mostly individual contributions, plus $5k from a QC-based company that was planning a while back to contribute.

That’s where it stalled. For almost 10 days, there was a plateau around $30k. Nobody said it aloud, but it was a belief that we’d reach $50k.

In the final week, there were a few emails exchanged though, and we decided to give a final push. We had a Tara Hunt video published, reached out to many companies, followed-up on leads, and got coverage on La Presse Affaires and many other outlets. And the magic happened! 3 days before the end, we had high profile contributors who gave in each $5k (or more) to get us to $100k. On the last day, we also had many individual contributors who saw the success and join the party.

 

LESSONS

What did we learn from the campaign? Lots were learned. It’s a great team work and that’s why we love crowdfunding. Here are a few other points:

  • It helps if you have a team backing you, otherwise you will be working on the campaign full-time, answering emails and twitter replies all day! Having also a pro HootSuite account helps, since you can also schedule and automate the campaign.
  • Provide images, videos, soundbites of the project. You don’t know at the beginning what’s attractive so make sure there are various renderings in the press kit. Having a press mention is key, because that creates credibility for your project and will get a lot of people to contribute.
  • Everyone loves shout-outs, especially on Facebook. Have a special message about the person, who they are, and appreciate their $10 or $25 contribution.
  • Having a main contributors page helps a lot, with various categories, especially when you announce that the page will be online forever. It’s the concept of the one million dollar page, with the feeling that you’ll be forever in the project history.
  • It’s essential to reach out to different communities. Call and meet them, explain the campaign and the tools, tell the vision, and ask them how they can communicate the vision to their members. Of course, it really helps if you are already full time
  • It’s essential to have one person in charge of the preparations (Video, press, launch) and to also one main person for the campaign itself. When there are so many things to do, you don’t want to loose track of a valuable lead or get the press cold about the campaign. So make sure you have a schedule for the team, someone knows where and how to lead the campaign, and also a large database of images & files to share to fans and anyone who wants them.
